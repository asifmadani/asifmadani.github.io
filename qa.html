Published Answers

What is the ruling on performing Salah in congregation (Jama'ah)?

+

Performing Salah in congregation is a strongly emphasised Sunnah (Sunnah Mu'akkadah) according to the majority of scholars, and some hold it to be obligatory (Wajib) upon men who are able to do so. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Salah in congregation is twenty-seven times more virtuous than Salah performed individually." (Bukhari & Muslim). One should endeavour to pray in the masjid whenever possible.

Is it permissible to seek knowledge through online platforms and digital books?

+

Yes, seeking knowledge through modern means — including online platforms, digital books, and recorded lectures — is permissible and indeed encouraged, as long as the content is authentic and drawn from reliable scholars. The obligation to seek knowledge does not restrict the means by which it is acquired. However, seeking the company of qualified teachers remains important, especially for advanced Islamic sciences, as knowledge is transmitted with blessing through qualified chains of scholarship.

How should a Muslim deal with doubt in faith (Waswas)?

+

Intrusive doubts (Waswas) are a tool of Shaytan and do not nullify faith. The Prophet ﷺ instructed: "Seek refuge in Allah and stop (entertaining it)." (Muslim). One should respond to doubts by: (1) seeking knowledge through authentic sources, (2) increasing dhikr (remembrance of Allah), (3) engaging in righteous company, and (4) not dwelling on or entertaining such thoughts. The very fact that one is disturbed by doubts is a sign of living faith in the heart.

What are the conditions for a valid Nikah (Islamic marriage)?

+

The essential conditions for a valid Nikah are: (1) Offer and Acceptance — clear proposal and acceptance from both parties; (2) Wali — the presence and consent of the bride's guardian; (3) Two Witnesses — at least two adult Muslim witnesses; (4) Mahr — a dowry agreed upon and given to the bride. Both parties must also be of sound mind and free from legal impediments (such as being related by blood within prohibited degrees). A Nikah conducted in secret without a Wali is invalid according to the majority of scholars.
